How the systems feed into each other

Every step pushes the planet through twelve pure systems in a fixed order. Solid-earth systems reshape the crust; the climate stack then solves on top of that terrain. Fast diagnostics are re-solved from scratch each step; three slow reservoirs — ice, sea level, CO₂ — close their loops back to the top with a deliberate one-step lag.

The step pipeline
The three feedback loops

Ice–albedo positive

ice→iceFraction→↺ next step energyBalance (albedo)→colder→more ice

White ice reflects sunlight, cooling the surface and growing more ice. Runaway is held off by a deliberately wide equilibrium-cover ramp that keeps d(albedo)/dT subcritical — but a strong cold shock still tips the whole planet into a reachable snowball.

CO₂ thermostat negative

carbon→co2→↺ next step energyBalance (greenhouse)→warmer→weathering→carbon

Warmth speeds silicate weathering, which draws CO₂ down and cools — a stabilizing loop. When ice seals the continents weathering stops, outgassing rebuilds CO₂, and a snowball thaws itself. This is what makes the ice loop recoverable.

Sea-level mask coupling

ice→grounded ice→seaLevel→seaLevelM→↺ next step energyBalance · erosion · moisture · ice · biome

Grounded ice locks water out of the ocean, dropping sea level and exposing shelves. The moved coastline becomes next step's land/ocean mask everywhere downstream — changing albedo, erosion base level, evaporation area and biomes.
